Meals
For Hope

The MOBI is Zimbabwe’s very first Mobile Soup Kitchen, providing nutritious meals and support to women and children in some of our most and isolated communities.

The MOBI was launched in October 2021 and is already providing its beneficiaries with crucial access to nutrition, support, education, healthcare and long term change.

The MOBI is an initiative that aims to reduce hunger and poverty in women and children, with a focus:

  • Food & nutrition
  • Health and psychosocial support
  • Education and literacy
  • Disaster/emergency response and relief
  • Awareness, fundraising and support of homeless artisans

THE MOBI

The MOBI currently provides in excess of 1,200 nutritious meals per month to children under 15 living in squatter camps in Hatcliffe. We believe that the most effective way of helping others is to understand their plight and gain their trust before forcing solutions onto them. The best way to achieve this is to spend time within specific communities, developing a relationship with them whilst assisting where we can. We strongly believe that, through the use of the MOBI, we will be able to develop this relationship and provide long term assistance to the people that we visit.

The Mobi is also equipped to react to crises involving at risk communities and provide disaster relief where needed and is a fixture at all Harare’s fairs, exhibitions and markets where it is used as a platform to raise awareness, fundraise and sell crafts, curios and artwork on behalf of our vulnerable communities.

History will judge "us by the difference we make in the everyday lives of children

AIMS OF THE MOBI

With the MOBI we are able to reach communities that have no access to alternative assistance through the various soup kitchens, Churches, and Charitable organisations operating in and around Harare, with a focus on children under the age of 15.


Mobi currently visits two major squatter camps in Hatcliffe each week, initially visiting the same communities in order to build up the important relationships required for effecting change. As our team builds up registers of our beneficiaries, we will begin to introduce partners to assist with specific issues within the communities, such as malnutrition, literacy and education, healthcare and general welfare, as well as HIV/AIDS treatment, support and education.


As the MOBI continues to grow, we will expand our reach into other communities such as Epworth and Hopely.
